04

About

I spent four years as a freelance technical editor and subtitle localizer for international clients — work that sustained more than a million content views and, less visibly, taught me to care about defects. When a single mistimed line is multiplied across an audience that size, "good enough" stops being good enough. That's where my instinct for QA came from, long before it had a job title.

Now I'm a final-year BSIT (Cybersecurity) student at Mapúa Malayan Colleges Laguna, working as a Functional Business Analyst. I like the part of the job most people skip: sitting with business users until the actual requirement is on the table, mapping how a process really works versus how people think it works, and writing it down precisely enough that engineers and auditors can both trust it.

Outside coursework I contribute to BetterGov.ph, building out the Calamba City transparency portal. I work on it because I care about how Philippine local government serves people — not because it looks good on a page. I'm looking for a role where I can keep living on the line between business and engineering: business analysis, QA, or systems work with real stakes.
